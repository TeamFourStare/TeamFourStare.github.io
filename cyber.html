Cyber Threats

What is a Cyber Attack?

A Cyber-attack is an individual or a group that targets computer information systems, Network, personal computer devices by various means of malicious acts.

Types of Attacks

  • Malware (most common threat) - Malware is a code created for the purpose to destroy something on a computer, steal private information.
  • Viruses - Makes a computer “sick” much like sickness to a human, they replicate and survive when attached to files or programs. They are often hard to find and to get rid of.
  • Spyware- a malware that spies or monitors their victims it can log the various activities performed by a user. Spyware is capable of recording keystrokes ( what a user types on the keyboard. It means that the attacker can view passwords the victim enters. Also used to steal confidential information.
  • Worms – worms are like viruses the replicate and infest. The only difference is that they don’t need to be attached to a file or program. They can survive by themselves, they just don’t spread in one computer, but can also expand through a network of computers.
  • Trojan – a malware (code) that disguise itself as a legitimate program. Users are trick to open one of the programs that can expose them to a program expose thme to loading and executing Trojans on their systems. Once activated, Trojans can enable cyber-criminals to spy on you, steal your sensitive data, and gain backdoor access to your system. They don’t replicate.

By installing effective anti-malware software, you can defend your devices – including PCs, laptops, Macs, tablets and smartphones – against Trojans. A rigorous anti-malware solution – such as Kaspersky Anti-Virus – will detect and prevent Trojan attacks on your PC, while Kaspersky Mobile Security can deliver world-class virus protection for Android smartphones.
