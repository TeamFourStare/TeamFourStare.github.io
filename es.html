Estefany Salcedo

"Uh, have you tried turning it on and off again?"

Estefany Salcedo or as I am better known within my community, the “young woman who could do anything.” This title comes from my ability to learn quickly and apply that to any setting. Like my father, I was raised to give every task my 300%. I set high standards for myself and I will not stop till those standards are met. Whether it is learning a new language to interpret for a neighbor or analyzing and surveying young pregnant or parenting youth's needs in order to advocate their rights within the school system. My experience working with non-profit organizations such as After School Matters (ASM), Illinois College Advising Corps (ICAC), and Illinois Caucus for Adolescent Health (ICAH) has further driven my passion in advocating for youth rights.

Prior to my current role as a Year Up student, I was a student at the University of Illinois at Chicago for two years, where I also worked as a receptionist for the University Student Financial Services Office of Projects and Operations (also known as USFSCO). My work with USFSCO has challenged me to further developing my customer service skills. It has allowed me to experience many instances where I was forced to maintain focus on multiple tasks and doing so in an efficient and effective manner.
